Paper City Curriculum

The Paper City Curriculum is composed of customized Help Increase the Peace (HIPP) lessons that incorporate the film's characters and themes as source material, and use City Year's values and graduation pipeline model to inspire youth to achieve the American Dream through education and social justice.

Help Increase the Peace Project

The Help Increase the Peace Project (HIPP) is a youth leadership program created by the Nobel Peace Prize-winning American Friends Service Committee (AFSC) in 1991. HIPP is an interactive program made to address issues of interpersonal violence, prejudice, and injustice with participants of all ages, especially middle- and high-school age youth.

HIPP uses examples from participants’ own life experiences, the news, and history to cultivate the power of decision-making, alternatives to violence, and the belief that we are all agents of positive social change.

City Year

City Year is an education-focused Americorps program purposed to combat the dropout crisis directly by building a graduation pipeline in over 200 schools in 25 US cities. City Year trains 17-24 year-old high school and college graduates to work in high needs schools as full-time tutors, mentors, and role models to help students with attendance, behavior, and coursework in math and English—pivotal factors toward graduation.
