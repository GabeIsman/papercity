Paper City

The film
This is a story of the American Dream in one of the nation’s arson capitals

Paper City began from interviews with middle school students who must navigate booming drug economies, struggling school systems, gang warfare, and unprecedented rates of mass incarceration in order to make it to graduation.

In response, a community of teachers, law officers, activists, and politicians created Paper City for youth to understand these issues and realize their power to determine the future in America.

Paper City is divided into 7 chapters that address specific points within the school-to-prison pipeline along with corresponding Help Increase the Peace (HIPP) lessons and activities.

These interactive lessons engage middle school, high school, and adult audiences in active discussion about socio-economics, community empowerment, restorative justice, and the power of decision-making.

  • The American Dream (3:39)
    Through the examples of Irish and Puerto Rican immigration into Holyoke, participants will question: what is the American Dream?
  • Pursuit of Happiness (5:30)
    In this lesson, participants will explore how they have the power to achieve the American Dream through education and social justice.
  • School System (9:45)
    School systems provide opportunity for upward mobility but suffer from socio-economic disparities. Here, participants will explore what it means to counteract social and economic inequality.
  • Drug Economy (8:01)
    This violent, illegal, industry provides economic mobility in low-income communities. Participants will learn to recognize the root causes of violence and find non-violent solutions to conflict.
  • War on Drugs (5:28)
    Police forces are fighting America’s longest running war in low-income communities. Here, participants will explore how violence works through discriminatory law.
  • Prison Industrial Complex (1:08)
    Prisons outrank schools for public funding. With this lesson, participants will question decision-making on a larger level: why is society making these choices?
  • Chaos or Community (8:16)
    What will you put your power towards—Chaos or Community? In this lesson, participants will recognize that they each have power to make choices and determine the future of America.
