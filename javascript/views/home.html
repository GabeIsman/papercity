Paper City

Your power will change the world
Inspired by Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.’s final book, Where Do We Go From Here: Chaos or Community?

Paper City is an educational program and 45-minute documentary intended for adolescent youth to learn about the American Dream and the school-to-prison pipeline – the nationwide phenomenon in which high school dropout rates coincide with mass incarceration of urban youth.

Created by educators from two national youth leadership programs - City Year and The Help Increase the Peace Project - this new program is designed to inspire youth and teachers alike to realize the American Dream as Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. envisioned. The central message of Paper City is this: you have power, and your power will change the world.

Paper City is designed to help youth, educators, and advocates counteract the school-to-prison pipeline through education by combining City Year’s graduation pipeline model with the Help Increase the Peace Project.

Paper City is divided into “chapter” segments that address specific points within the school-to-prison pipeline with corresponding Help Increase the Peace (HIPP) activities and lessons. These lessons engage middle, high school, and adult audiences in active discussion about socio-economics, community empowerment, restorative justice, and the power of decision-making.
