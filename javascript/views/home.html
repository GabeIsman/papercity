Paper City

Your power will change the world
Inspired by Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.’s final book, Where Do We Go From Here: Chaos or Community?

Paper City is an educational program and 45-minute documentary about the American Dream and the school-to-prison pipeline – the nationwide phenomenon in which high school dropout rates coincide with mass incarceration of urban youth.

Created by educators from two national youth leadership programs - City Year and the Help Increase the Peace Project - this new program is designed to inspire youth and teachers alike to realize the American Dream as Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. envisioned.

The Problem

The United States has 5% of the world’s population, but holds 25% of the world’s prisoners. Source: Paper City

50% of the 800,000 students who drop out every year come from 10% of the nation’s schools. Source: City Year

Paper City is provided for free to help youth, educators, and advocates counteract the school to prison pipeline by combining City Year's graduation pipeline model with the Help Increase the Peace Project.

The Solution

Paper City is divided into “chapter” segments that address specific points within the school-to-prison pipeline with corresponding Help Increase the Peace (HIPP) activities and lessons. These lessons engage middle school, high school, and adult audiences in active discussions about socio-economics, community empowerment, restorative justice, and the power of decision-making.
